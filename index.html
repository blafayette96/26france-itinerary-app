Primer — why these whites
Bordeaux is famous for reds, but the dry whites of Pessac-Léognan are its quiet flex — Sauvignon Blanc for zip, Sémillon for weight, usually barrel-fermented so a faint toast sits under the citrus. Smith Haut Lafitte's white is the benchmark: mineral and precise like a Sancerre, but layered and rounder. Taste it against Domaine de Chevalier's and you'll feel the house-to-house difference that makes Bordeaux worth studying. For Sam, who leans mineral whites, this is the region speaking her language.Read more ↗

Primer — the Médoc & the back-vintage pour
The Médoc is Cabernet Sauvignon country — deep gravel banks that drain hard and ripen the grape fully, giving structured, cellar-worthy reds. Lynch-Bages is a Pauillac fifth-growth that has long overdelivered for its rank, which is exactly why an older, resolved bottle is the closest thing to the aged Caymus you both love. Cos d'Estournel next door earns the stop for the wine and the theater: a 19th-century château topped with pagodas, built by a merchant obsessed with the East. Two estates, one lunch, and the classified-growth system starts to make sense in the glass.Read more ↗

Primer — Gordes, Sénanque & the ochre country
Gordes stacks up its hillside in pale limestone — the postcard Provençal village. Just below, the 12th-century Sénanque Abbey is a lesson in Cistercian restraint: no ornament, all proportion, lavender out front (past peak by September, but still the classic frame). Roussillon is the opposite key entirely — an entire village built in ochre, the pigment quarried right beside it. Short drives, big contrast: austerity, then saturated color.Read more ↗

Primer — shopping the brocante & faïence
Isle-sur-la-Sorgue is one of Europe's great antiques towns — 300+ permanent dealers, open Friday through Monday. For the hand-painted ceramics Sam wants — the Moustiers / Positano look, white ground with botanical and floral motifs — you'll find antique faïence here. If she'd rather buy new and ship home, the Moustiers ateliers (Atelier Soleil, Faïence Bondil) match the style exactly and ship to the US, so nothing has to fit in a suitcase. Buy by eye, not by list — that's how she shops anyway.Read more ↗
